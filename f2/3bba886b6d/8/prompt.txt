## PHASE 2: THE "DIGITAL RUINS" DESIGN FRAMEWORK (STRICT RULES)
Every line of CSS or component code must strictly adhere to these principles:

### Materials & Atmosphere
* **The Void as Canvas:** Base background is near-black (`bg-[#0a0a0a]` to `bg-[#050505]`). No pure black — always hint at depth, like staring into a CRT in a dark room.
* **Phosphor Glass Layers:** Translucent containers use `backdrop-blur-xl` combined with ultra-low opacity greens and teals (`bg-emerald-500/5`, `bg-cyan-500/8`, `bg-green-400/3`). Glass panels should feel like HUD overlays projected onto nothing.
* **The Rain:** Subtle, perpetual background animation — a sparse cascade of falling characters/glyphs (katakana, thin monospace fragments) at ~3-5% opacity. Never distracting, always atmospheric. This is the persistent "digital rain" layer beneath all content.
* **Scan Lines & Grain:** A faint CSS scan-line overlay (`repeating-linear-gradient` at 1-2px intervals, ~2% opacity) and subtle film grain give every surface the texture of a CRT monitor or visor display.
* **Ambient Glow Bleed:** Key UI regions emit soft, diffused color light into their surroundings — like bioluminescent organisms in Metroid's caves. Use radial gradients and box-shadows with `emerald/cyan/amber` at very low opacity, high spread.

### Color System — "Chozo Terminal Palette"
* **Primary Glow:** Electric green / emerald (`#00ff41`, `emerald-400/500`) — the Matrix's signature. Used for active states, primary actions, key data.
* **Secondary Signal:** Teal / cyan (`cyan-400`, `#0abdc6`) — Metroid's visor-scan color. Used for navigation, secondary info, scan/highlight states.
* **Warning/Power:** Amber / orange (`amber-400`, `#ff6600`) — Samus's arm cannon charge. Used for alerts, destructive actions, energy states.
* **Danger/Critical:** Deep red (`red-500/600`, `#ff0033`) — hull breach, low health. Used sparingly for errors and critical warnings.
* **Text Hierarchy:**
    - Primary text: `text-green-300/90` or `text-emerald-100/80` — bright enough to read, soft enough to not burn.
    - Secondary text: `text-green-400/40` — faded terminal output.
    - Ghost/disabled text: `text-green-900/30` — barely visible, like dead pixels.

### Typography — "Monospace is Law"
* **Primary Font:** `JetBrains Mono`, `Fira Code`, `IBM Plex Mono`, or equivalent. Everything is monospaced — this is a terminal, not a magazine.
* **Display/Headers:** Can use a condensed sans-serif like `Chakra Petch`, `Orbitron`, or `Rajdhani` for section headers to evoke Metroid's UI overlays. Always uppercase, always tracked wide (`tracking-[0.2em]`+).
* **Text Rendering:** Apply a subtle `text-shadow` in the glow color (`0 0 8px rgba(0,255,65,0.3)`) to all primary text. Text should feel like it's being projected, not printed.
* **Hierarchy through opacity and glow intensity**, not size variation. Keep sizing tight and uniform.

### Borders, Containers & Spatial Logic
* **Borders Glow, Not Divide:** Borders are `border border-emerald-500/10` at rest, brightening to `border-emerald-400/30` on hover. They should feel like faintly powered circuit traces.
* **Corner Geometry:** Use sharp or subtly clipped corners (`rounded-sm` or `rounded-none` with decorative corner accents) — not friendly rounded blobs. Think Metroid's HUD frames: angular, deliberate, architectural.
* **Decorative Corner Brackets:** Key panels get small `::before`/`::after` pseudo-element corner marks — like targeting reticles or blueprint anchors. Thin 1px lines in the glow color, 8-12px long, positioned at corners.
* **Negative Space as Desolation:** Generous padding/margins, but it should feel *empty* and *vast*, not "airy and friendly." The whitespace is the vacuum of space or the silence of an abandoned station.

### Shadows & Depth
* **No traditional drop shadows.** Instead, use **glow shadows**: `shadow-[0_0_15px_rgba(0,255,65,0.07)]` for resting state, intensifying on hover/focus. Objects emit light, they don't cast darkness.
* **Layered glow:** Combine a tight inner glow with a wide diffused outer glow for important elements (buttons, active cards).

### Motion & Animation — "Everything Breathes"
* **No instant transitions.** Everything uses custom easing: `cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.46, 0.45, 0.94)` for smooth mechanical movement, or spring physics for interactive elements.
* **Idle Pulse:** Key elements (active nav items, primary buttons, status indicators) have a slow, rhythmic opacity pulse or glow oscillation — like a heartbeat or a reactor hum. `animation: pulse 4s ease-in-out infinite`.
* **Reveal Animations:** Content entering the viewport should "boot up" — a brief flicker, a scan-line sweep, or a typewriter text reveal. Not flashy, but enough to feel like the UI is *powering on*.
* **Hover = Scan:** On hover, elements should feel like they're being scanned or targeted — a brief brightness increase, a border glow-up, maybe a subtle horizontal scan-line pass.
* **Page Transitions:** Cross-fade with a brief CRT-static or glitch frame (2-3 frames of subtle distortion, then clean resolve). Like Samus entering a new room.

### Iconography & Visual Language
* **Minimal line icons**, thin stroke, in the glow color. No filled icons, no emoji. Think wireframe schematics.
* **Status indicators** should resemble Metroid's HUD pips — small geometric shapes (diamonds, thin bars) that fill or glow based on state.
* **Data visualization** should feel like sonar/radar — circular, gridded, with sweep animations.

## PHASE 3: SEQUENTIAL RE-ARCHITECTURE
Execute the refactoring in this precise order:

1. **The Void (Foundation):**
   Strip out the old theme entirely. Implement the dark base environment: near-black background, scan-line overlay, subtle digital rain layer, film grain texture. Set up the monospace typographic scale and the glow color tokens.

2. **The Hull (Glass Layer):**
   Rewrite all layout containers, modals, sidebars, and navigation to the phosphor-glass spec. Every panel is a translucent HUD element floating in the void. Add decorative corner brackets to primary containers.

3. **The Interface (Component Subtraction & Rebuild):**
   Redesign `<Input>`, `<Select>`, `<Button>`, and all interactive components:
   - Inputs: Borderless at rest, faint underline or bottom-glow. On focus, the border circuit-traces illuminate. Text cursor should glow.
   - Buttons: Primary buttons get the emerald glow treatment (glowing border, subtle fill, text-shadow). Secondary buttons are ghost/wireframe only. All buttons pulse subtly on idle if they represent a primary action.
   - Selects/Dropdowns: Dark glass panels that materialize downward with a brief flicker animation.

4. **The Signal (State Polish):**
   - Focus rings: Animated glow rings that expand outward briefly then settle (like a sonar ping).
   - Hover states: Brightness shift + scan-line sweep.
   - Active/pressed: Brief inward flash, like a circuit completing.
   - Loading states: Horizontal scan-line animation or dot-matrix pattern fill.
   - Error states: Red glow bleed with a subtle screen-shake (1-2px, 100ms).

5. **The Atmosphere (Final Layer):**
   - Add ambient background glow sources (large, soft radial gradients in emerald/cyan that drift slowly).
   - Ensure the digital rain layer is properly z-indexed behind all content.
   - Add a subtle vignette (radial gradient darkening at edges) to the viewport.
   - Verify all glow effects composite cleanly without performance degradation.
